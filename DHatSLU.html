Public Humanities Oriented Projects

Lived Religion in the Digital Age
A project seeking to better understand the sights, sounds, tastes, rituals, beliefs, and overall experiences of religion in the everyday lives of practitioners.
Nuisance Laws and Battered Women
>
After learning that the city of St. Louis was evicting domestic violence victims from their homes under the nuisance property law and that this was a widespread practice around the country, Dr. Arnold conducted multiple academic studies, including interviewing victims of the law, primarily low-income women of color. The nuisancelaws.org project was created to bring the academic scholarship to a wider audience, especially lawmakers who might consider changing their nuisance laws or rethink implementing a nuisance law in their own community.

Text Analysis Projects

Glossing Matthew
The project aims to compile marginal and interlinear glosses preserved in 12th-century manuscripts of the Gospel of Matthew. The project uses glosses on Matthew 5 as a test case for the development of DH tools that would facilitate the collection, presentation, and study of glosses on authoritative texts in medieval Europe. The DH tools could have application in multiple historical, philosophical, and theological subfields for many cultures and manuscripts.
